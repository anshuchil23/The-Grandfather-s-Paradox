He looks at me with a soft smile. A smile I thought I'd never see. " Tea helps calm the nerves ", he notes as he pours me a cup of fragrant Darjeeling tea from a rustic china teapot. I hold the cup in my hand. I blow the steam off. I take a sip. The warmth flows through my mouth, down my throat. I feel relaxed. I shouldn't. Not right now. Lester's room felt so alien. The wooden dining table; probably oak, or walnut, maybe mahogany. A Turkish carpet hiding under it, alongside bits of crackers. The room was not extravagant. It was self-sufficient. The window opening the house to an overwhelmingly bright, but beautiful countryside green. The light inside the house, refracted through Lester's beakers and test tubes, painted strokes on the wall. It was all so alien, but the Tea flowed through me and I felt at home. " So what's the future like? ", he asks with the glimmering eyes of a curious four year old.

" I don't think it's a good idea to give you details. It could screw up the Time Continuum. "
