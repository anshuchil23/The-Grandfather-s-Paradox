" I'm sorry Lester ", I say. " This is the first time I'm meeting you ". His brows frown. He looks at me and asks, " Do I die before you're born? ". I found that funny, so I chuckled and replied, " Don't worry Lester you died much later ". I looked at him expecting a laugh. Lester was not laughing. He wore a serious look on his face, almost as if disappointed. " You die in your eighties, Lester, there is nothing to worry about ", I remark. He looks at me and says, " Doesn't that make it worse? I didn't even make the effort to meet you, my own grandson ". He pauses, sighs, and mutters under his breath " Classic old me ". I felt bad. Maybe I should have lied to him after all. There was no reason for Lester to die filled with regret. Lester walked towards the kitchen. He opens a drawer and picks out what seems like a photograph. He brings it to the table, but doesn't show it to me. "And how about your grandmother? ", he asks me. " Oh she was the only one who backed you up when you weren't around. Grandma lived a happy life and passed peacefully at the age of ninety one", I replied. He swayed his fingers across the picture and smiled. After a pause, Lester asks, " When is your birthday? ". How did he know. There was no way he could have known. " Today ", I reply. He smiled again. "Then let me do this for you. This is my gift to you; for all the birthday parties I missed.", he says. With a hint of disapproval, I reply, " Killing your grandfather isn't really the best birthday gift ". He laughs. I laugh. The laughter dies and then he signals to me with a nod that he is ready. I take out my blaster and aim it at him. He tells me, " Thank you for letting me do this ". I shoot him. Lester dies.
